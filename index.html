Introduction

I am driven by a deep curiosity on how the universe works, and I am developing tools to answer these questions. Currently a postdoctoral researcher at the FORTH Institute of Astrophysics in Crete, Greece. My PhD in astrophysics was earned from the university of Cologne, which I worked on at the Max-Planck-Institute for Radio Astronomy in Bonn, Germany.

My research interests mainly concern the study of active galactic nuclei and relativistic jets, especially in the radio-band and with polarimetry.

In my current work, I specialize on data reduction and analysis of radio-interferometric data to obtain high-resolution images of jets in active galactic nuclei, with the goal to understand the physical properties of these objects. In my current positions in the SMILE project, we aim specifically to use these observations to constrain the properties of dark matter through dark matter halos acting as gravitational lenses.

Due to my work I have acquired extensive experience and expertise in programming, imaging, as well as handling and analyzing large amount of data.

Projects

  • SMILE

    Search for milli-lenses to discriminate between dark matter models.

  • RadioAstron

    Space-based very-long-baseline interferometry reveals the innermost regions of jets in activate galactic nuclei.

    Resolving the event-horizon scales of supermassive black holes in the centre of galaxies.
